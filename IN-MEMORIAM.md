# In Memoriam — Natalie Sun

> *"It's okay to have a million different voices and different emotions; you just need to try and find the balance."*
> — Natalie Sun

**Natalie Sun** was an artist and creative technologist who, at 25, turned a breast-cancer
diagnosis into one of the most honest and quietly profound pieces of personal storytelling on the
web: **Texting With Cancer** ([textingwithcancer.com](http://textingwithcancer.com)).

This repository is a faithful restoration of that website, rebuilt so her work stays online.
Natalie passed away in early 2021. This site is kept as a living memorial — to her humor, her
honesty, and her refusal to let fear have the last word.

## The project

On **July 31, 2015**, Natalie was diagnosed with breast cancer. Instead of a conventional blog, she
rendered the experience the way it actually feels from the inside — as a stream of text messages
between three voices:

- **Pink** — Natalie herself
- **White** — optimism
- **Gray** — pessimism

Across **584 messages** from **July 2015 to February 2016**, those three voices argue, joke,
despair, and comfort one another through diagnosis, chemo, hair loss, fear, dating, and the small
reclaimed joys of ordinary life. It is funny and unflinching in the same breath. The conversation
ends, deliberately, on hope:

> It's all about just having the confidence to know that you're going to be just fine. It'll work itself out.
> Besides, what's the worst that could happen?
> Honestly.
> **It's time to go live life.**

The project was inspired by *Before We Met*, built with a lot of help from
[Laith Azzam](http://laithazzam.com/), and made possible by her family, friends, and the art + tech
community she loved.

  keeping with the Webbys' famous five-word-speech tradition, Natalie gave a five-word acceptance
  speech — [watch it here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wc31uth25jQ). Fittingly, the speech was
  crowdsourced from the project's own community.
- **NPR / WAMU**, May 28, 2016 — *After Breast Cancer Diagnosis, She Channeled Her Ups And Downs
  Into Texts.*
- Featured by **The Next Web**, the **Daily Mirror**, **iHadCancer**, **The Breast Cancer Site**,
  and **Think Dirty**, among others.
